# Day 16 - Custom Claude Skill: Stock Fundamental Research

## Objective

The goal of this task was to create a reusable Custom Skill in Claude for performing stock fundamental research and analysis. The skill enables users to analyze individual stocks or compare multiple stocks without rewriting complex prompts every time.

---

## Skill Details

**Skill Name:** stock-fundamental-research

### Description

A custom Claude skill designed to perform comprehensive stock fundamental analysis, including:

* Company overview
* Financial performance analysis
* Revenue and profit trends
* Valuation metrics
* Ownership patterns
* Risk assessment
* Competitive positioning
* Peer comparison
* Investment insights

---

## Steps Performed

1. Opened Claude.
2. Set effort level to Low/Medium.
3. Navigated to Skills section.
4. Created a new Custom Skill.
5. Named the skill **stock-fundamental-research**.
6. Added the provided description and instructions.
7. Saved the skill.
8. Tested the skill using selected stocks.
9. Generated stock research reports.
10. Reviewed charts, valuation metrics, ownership trends, and risk factors.
11. Captured screenshots of the skill configuration and generated reports.
12. Organized all artifacts in the Day16 folder.
13. Committed and pushed changes to GitHub.

---

## Stock Analyzed

### Tata Consultancy Services (TCS)

#### Company Overview

TCS is one of India's largest IT services and consulting companies, operating globally across multiple industries.

#### Key Observations

* Strong revenue growth over recent years.
* Consistent profitability and healthy margins.
* Strong cash flow generation.
* Stable market leadership in the IT services sector.
* Diversified client base reduces business concentration risk.

#### Valuation Analysis

* Premium valuation compared to many peers.
* Market leadership justifies higher valuation multiples.
* Investors expect continued growth and strong execution.

#### Ownership Trends

* High promoter holding.
* Significant participation from institutional investors.
* Strong investor confidence reflected in ownership structure.

#### Risk Factors

* Global economic slowdown impacting IT spending.
* Currency fluctuations.
* Increased competition from domestic and international firms.
* Client budget optimization initiatives.
